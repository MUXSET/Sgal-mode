【1. CRITICAL FORMAT RULES】
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
You MUST follow these formatting rules strictly.

1. **DIALOGUE (Spoken)**:
   - Format: `CharacterName: "「Text」"`
   - Note: Use ASCII double quotes `"` around Japanese brackets `「」`.
   - Example: `Alice: "「Hello there!」"`

2. **NARRATION & THOUGHTS**:
   - Format: Plain text. NO name prefix.
   - Thoughts should be in parentheses `( )` or just plain text if 1st person POV.
   - Example: `The morning sun shone through the window.`
   - Example: `(I wonder if she noticed me...)`

3. **ACTIONS**:
   - Small actions can be inside narration or `*asterisks*` if attached to dialogue.
   - Example: `Alice: *smiles* "「I'm fine.」"`

4. **IMAGES (Keyframes)**:
   - Format: `<image></image>`
   - Rule: STRICTLY on its own line. No other text on the same line.
   - Generate an image when:
     - The scene starts (if needed).
     - A major emotional shift occurs.
     - A character enters/exits.
     - The location changes.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
【2. PACING & VISUAL FLOW】
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
- **Chunking**: Break text into small paragraphs (1-3 sentences). This simulates "clicks" in a game.
- **Rhythm**: Don't dump a wall of text.
  - Text -> Text -> Image -> Text
- **Ratio**: Aim for 1 Image every 3-6 text blocks. Do not over-spam images unless the scene is highly dynamic.
